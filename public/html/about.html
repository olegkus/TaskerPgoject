Before filling out this form, you should take some sensible precautions. We want to keep you safe and ensure that your contact with SIS is secure. Internet traffic is monitored by most governments. It is also possible, in some circumstances, for specialists to retrieve internet traffic from a device that you have used. To use this site safely, you should be confident in using the internet in a secure way and have a good understanding of the vulnerabilities of the device and internet connection you are using. We recommend you consider the following steps: Do not contact us from a computer or connection that can be linked to you or people that you know. Do use a new, disposable device or public computer (perhaps in a library or internet café – although in many counties these are closely monitored). If possible, do not contact us from inside your own country, or from a country likely to share security information with your country. Be aware of security cameras and other techniques that may allow other to see your screen. Create a new email account (for example Gmail, Outlook or GMX) for us to reply to and only check this account on a computer and connection not linked to you. Do not use a name, phone number or other details linked to you when creating this account. Consider additional precautions such as hiding your internet activity using commercial options. Commercial internet security is a fast changing field and you need to be sure any method you use is secure and does not attract attention. If you are unsure about any of the above, or believe that your contact with SIS through the internet could be intercepted and therefore cause you difficulties, please consider other options such as visiting a British Embassy in person, ideally in a country which has friendly relations with the UK. Threats to the United Kingdom The UK continues to face a growing number of global threats and challenges. SIS (MI6) mission includes gathering intelligence to counteract these threats. If you have any information that can help our mission then please use our secure contact us form. If you have information relating to immediate threats to the UK, such as terrorism, please pass this immediately to the Security Service. If you are living within the UK and you have information that relates to an imminent threat to life or property, please contact the police on 999 or the police Anti-Terrorist Hotline on 0800 789 321.
